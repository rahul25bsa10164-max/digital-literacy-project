# Task 4: Professional Email & Etiquette Guide

## Part C: Reflection Report
**Topic: The Impact of Poor Digital Communication**

A common situation where poor digital communication causes significant problems is the "Reply All" mistake in a professional office. For instance, an employee might accidentally send a private disagreement or a sensitive question about their salary to an entire company listserv instead of just their manager. This error leads to a major privacy breach, clutters dozens of inboxes, and causes immediate professional embarrassment.

To avoid this, the sender should have double-checked the "To" and "Cc" fields before clicking send. Furthermore, using a clear subject line and a formal greeting helps set a professional tone that encourages the sender to be more mindful. In digital spaces, "thinking before clicking" is the most effective strategy to maintain a positive online reputation and ensure that messages are received with the intended respect and clarity.
