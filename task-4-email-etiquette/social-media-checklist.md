 ✅ DO (Best Practices)
1.Privacy Check: Regularly review your privacy settings to control who can see your posts and personal information.

2.Think Before You Post: Ask yourself if you’d be comfortable with a future employer, professor, or parent seeing the content.

3.Verify Information: Check the credibility of a source before sharing news or "facts" to avoid spreading misinformation.

4.Network Professionally: Use platforms like LinkedIn to connect with alumni, professors, and industry professionals.

5.Be Respectful: Treat others with the same kindness and respect online as you would in a face-to-face conversation.

❌ DON'T (Common Pitfalls)
1.Overshare Private Info: Avoid posting your home address, current precise location, or phone number publicly.

2.Post During "Heat": Never post or reply when you are angry or upset; give yourself a "cool-down" period first.

3.Plagiarize Content: Don’t claim others' work, photos, or ideas as your own; always give proper credit.

4.Badmouth Your Institution: Avoid venting about professors or your university publicly, as this can appear unprofessional to recruiters.

5.Ignore Security: Don’t use the same password for all accounts or click on suspicious links sent via Direct Messages (DMs).
